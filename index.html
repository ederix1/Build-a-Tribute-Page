Roman Empire

The Empire that lasted 500 years

Timeline of the Roman Empire

  • 753 a.C - Founding of Rome
  • 752 a.C - Victory of Romulus over the Caeninians; first recorded Roman Triumph.
  • 716 a.C - Death of Romulus; Numa Pompilius assumes the throne a year later and reforms the Roman calendar.
  • 673 a.C - Tullus Hostilius assumes the throne. During his reign the duel of the Horatii and the destruction of Alba Longa, a rival city of Rome, took place.
  • 641 a.C - Anco Marcius assumes the throne after the death of Tullus Hostilius.
  • 616 a.C - Lucius Tarquinius Priscus assumes the throne after the death of Anco Marcius.
  • 578 a.C - Servius Tullius assumes the throne after the assassination of Lucius Tarquinius.
  • 566 a.C - First census in Rome: 84,700.
  • 543 a.C - Tarquin the Proud assumes the throne after assassinating Servius Tullius.
  • 507 a.C - Censo en Roma: 130,909.
  • 509 a.C - Overthrow of the monarchy and exile of Tarquin the Proud.
